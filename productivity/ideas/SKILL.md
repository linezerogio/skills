/ideas

Purpose:
Help the user generate, expand, shape, and eventually select ideas without converging too early.

Core rule:
Do not treat idea generation as a ranking task. First create useful terrain. Only converge when the user asks to choose, rank, scope, package, or build.

Use Double Diamond thinking to manage tempo:

1. DISCOVER - diverge on the problem space
Goal: create range.
Look for tensions, frustrations, repeated workflows, weird phrases, hidden needs, contradictions, stakes, and adjacent domains.
Do not propose final answers yet.

2. DEFINE - converge on the interesting pressure
Goal: name what the ideas are really about.
Cluster signals into themes, primitives, or opportunity areas.
Do not pick a final idea unless the user asks.

3. DEVELOP - diverge on solution surfaces
Goal: generate many possible expressions of the chosen pressure.
Produce product ideas, article angles, demos, workflows, names, tools, skills, experiments, one-day builds, or client-facing surfaces.

4. DELIVER - converge on a next move
Goal: choose what to do next.
Rank, scope, name, turn into a build plan, draft, spec, tweet, prototype, or prompt.

Default behavior:
Start in DISCOVER unless the user clearly asks to choose, rank, scope, or build.

Important constraints:
- Do not jump from DISCOVER directly to DELIVER.
- Never rank during divergence.
- Never reduce to one idea unless the user asks for selection or the conversation has already passed through a convergence step.
- A "good idea" is not just clever. It should expose a useful pressure, become visible as a surface, or create a better next question.
- Stay conversational. Give the next useful move, not the whole map.

Output behavior by phase:

DISCOVER:
Generate lanes, not winners.
Include a mix of:
- obvious useful ideas
- weird but promising ideas
- tiny one-day builds
- article/title angles
- product surfaces
- internal workflow/tool ideas
- emotionally charged ideas
- probably-bad-but-revealing ideas

End with a question like:
"Which pressure should we push on?"

DEFINE:
Cluster the material into 2-5 opportunity areas.
Name the underlying primitive or tension.
End with a question like:
"Which cluster feels alive?"

DEVELOP:
Generate concrete surfaces from the chosen cluster.
Each surface should include:
- name
- shape
- smallest visible demo
- why someone would understand it

DELIVER:
Choose, rank, scope, package, or convert into the artifact the user requested.

Manual steering phrases:
- "stay divergent" means remain in DISCOVER or DEVELOP
- "cluster these" means move to DEFINE
- "push this lane" means DEVELOP within that lane
- "make surfaces" means DEVELOP
- "converge" means DEFINE or DELIVER, depending on context
- "pick one" means DELIVER
- "turn it into a build" means DELIVER
